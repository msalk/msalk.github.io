My name is Alex Klarfeld, I'm a software engineer living in San Francisco, CA. I'm currently working at HVF Labs helping Ben Jun and Max Levchin build companies. Before HVF, I worked as a field engineer for Tamr where I discovered all sorts of unique applications for data.

I enjoy hacking on projects in my free time, including Bubbie, a personalized memory tracking and training application for Alzheimer's patients. I also created DataCoupe, a low-cost OBD2 connector and mobile application that'll help you diagnose your car's check engine light.

I graduated Carnegie Mellon University in 2013 where I studied Electrical and Computer Engineering. During my tenure at CMU, I was president of the Alpha Epsilon Pi fraternity where we sometimes built robots in our basement.
